What is GSAP?

The GSAP animation platform is composed of four main elements. TweenLite, TweenMax, TimelineLite, and TimelineMax. As the name suggests, TweenLite and TweenMaxare very similar, and TimelineLiteand TimelineMax are very similar. The lite versions run fast and very smoothly max version is more of an extension of the lite version. It includes more functionalities. Whereas TweenLite and TweenMax are designed to animate one thing, TimelineLite and TimelineMax is designed as containers for multiple animations. If you have multiple tweens you want to animate, you will use either TimelineLite or TimelineMax.

TweenMax and stagger

TweenMax extends TweenLite which means you can you anything TweenLite can do and more. It adds usuful features like repeat() yoyo() and etc. And when you are working with TweenMax, you don't have to manually add plugins like CSSPlugin and AttrPlugin. In the next example. Multiple Tweening

GSAP really shines for complex sequencing of animation. With TimelineLite, you can manage multiple tweens in a incredibly simple way. Also, it's easy to later edit your animation. Here is a constuctor with tweens chained with to()methods. var tl = new TimelineLite(); tl.to(element, 1, {left:100}).from(element, 2, {top:50}).to(element, 1, {opacity:0});
